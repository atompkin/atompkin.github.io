Bio

Andrew Tompkins is a digital artist who creates immersive experiences. Andrew utilizes 3D modeling, 360 video, and drone based photography to create unique pieces. He is currently pursing a degree in Digital Arts at Stetson University. He has plans to work at Clemson University over the summer researching virtual field trips.

My Work

Senior Project

My senior objective is to create an interactive 360 environment. I want to utilize the HTC Vive as the platform used to interact with the environment. The 360 environment will be built in the Unity3D engine. The environment will be made using a combination of 3D modeling and 360 Video. I learned how to do 3D modeling from Dr. Chen’s 3D Modelling & Animation. I am experimenting with 360 video in the Advance Digital Studio class and will continue to work with 360 video over the summer while participating in a REU at Clemson University. During my time at Clemson, I will also become much more familiar with the Unity3D engine since it is the platform I will be utilizing in my research over the summer. I will have some basic understanding of the Unity3D engine from Dr. Chen’s final assignment of the semester. One group of artists that inspire me to use 360 video for my project is Corridor Digital. They are known for visual effects and have made 360 video experiences using their visual effects background. Another group that I found inspirational was Wevr: a small company that specializes in creating VR experiences and is best known for the experience called theBlu: Encounter. The examples given are just a small collection of all of the 360 environments that are available today. The 360 environment project will give me the skills and an incredibly portfolio piece that shows off the multifaceted digital artist I am.
